Welcome back to Strange Nature — I'm your host, and today: the octopus.

An octopus has three hearts. Two pump blood through the gills, and one drives it around the body. That blood runs blue, because it carries oxygen with copper instead of iron.

Of its roughly five hundred million neurons, nearly two thirds live not in its head, but in its arms — each arm tastes, touches, and partly decides on its own.

And here's the strangest part: an octopus is functionally colorblind. Yet it can match the color and texture of coral, sand, or stone in under a second, using thousands of pigment cells in its skin called chromatophores.
